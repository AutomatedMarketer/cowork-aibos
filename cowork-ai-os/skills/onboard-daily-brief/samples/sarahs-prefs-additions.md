## Q5 — Tasks (today.md) rules

My task file lives at `projects/daily-brief/today.md`.

I update it Sunday night for the week and tweak each morning. Format:

```markdown
# Today — Tuesday May 5

- [ ] Review PR #47 [QUICK WIN]
- [ ] Send Acme Q3 retainer proposal [OVERDUE]
- [ ] Record 3 Loom videos for new client onboarding

# Parking Lot — This Week

- [ ] Wed: Retainer renewal call with Jenny
- [ ] Thu: Update sales page copy
- [ ] Fri: Sprint planning with Mike + Lisa
```

The brief surfaces only unchecked items under the "Today" header, in the order I wrote them. Surface `[QUICK WIN]` and `[OVERDUE]` tags inline. Do NOT surface anything from the Parking Lot section.

## Q6 — 90-day outcome (ranking lens)

**Sign 3 new $4,997 retainer clients by August 1.**

Anything that moves a prospect closer to signature — fielded reply, contract sent, demo booked, proposal sent — outranks everything else in the brief.

Operations stuff (failed payments, contractor questions, client deliverables) ranks second. Internal admin (taxes, software renewals, logistics) ranks last.

When in doubt, the prospect-conversion item wins.

## Q7 — Priority line tone refinement

**Chief of staff tone.** One sentence. Direct, slightly blunt, no hedging.

**Must reference one of the highlights above it** — the priority line points at something already in the brief, never a vague "be productive" line.

**Examples that hit:**
- "Address scope creep with Jenny before the retainer talk."
- "Re-send the Stripe invoice to Acme before the 10 AM — don't open the renewal cold."
- "Knock out PR #47 first — Mike's blocked until you review."

**Examples that miss:**
- "Have a great day! 🌅" (cheerleading)
- "You may want to consider thinking about your priorities today" (hedging)
- "Focus on the most important thing" (vague)

## Q8 — Stricter draft rules

### When to draft

Draft a reply IF the email meets ALL these criteria:
- Surfaced in Urgent Inbox (matches my "always matter" list from P01)
- Requires a response (asks a question, requests a decision, or invites a meeting)
- Hasn't been replied to in the last 24h
- Maximum 3 drafts per brief

### When NOT to draft

- Anything ambiguous about what I want to say
- Anything requiring information not in my context (specific numbers, decisions only I can authorize)
- Anything needing more than a 4-paragraph reply
- First-time outreach from an unknown sender
- Sensitive topics: legal, compensation, hiring/firing

### Tone

Use rules from `about-me/writing-rules.md`. Match the sender's energy — formal client = formal reply, casual contractor = casual reply.

### Format

Plain text. No markdown. Maximum 4 paragraphs. Sign-off matches writing-rules.md sign-off rules.
